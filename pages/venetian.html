3RD YEAR PROJECT | ST. APOSTLI, VENICE

Venetian Fields

Mixed Use Building

The brief was to design a mixed use contemporary building that sits within a Venetian Campo (or square). Functions include a gondola workshop, gelateria, tavern, pizzeria, medical centre and housing. The site, Campo Santi Apostoli was one much historical significance as it contains one of Venice’s oldest churches. The proposal is a solid concrete base that engages with the campo by skewing the axis such that it engages more with the venetian routes and the church. Through a transfer structure sit the remaining 3 floors of building, primarily made of CLT with glulam columns for support. Campo Santi Apostoli exists in many layers. The interplay between the various realms accent the complexity of human interaction in the space. The building is a metaphor for the layered nature of the campo. Sitting inside a perforated metal shell is a building that operates within itself. It adds to the visual enticement of the development creating an interplay with light ad the various elements of nature. Capping it off is a dynamic shutter system that adds another layer of complexity to the facade. This allows the building the not only live and thrive inside but adapt to the external environment.
