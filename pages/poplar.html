3RD YEAR PROJECT | POPLAR, LONDON

Project Poplar

Bridge and Public Building

The brief was a development and bridge in Poplar, a borough in London. Poplar HARCA (Housing and regeneration community association) were looking to unlock the potential of the site available to us. It was a unique opportunity to connect different communities that are currently ‘cut’ off by the Limehouse cut canal. The site is at the centre of Poplar’s most engaging community projects and schools. When Chrisp Street Market realises its full potential the site will become a thoroughfare to the North of the Cut and thus a key destination for the wider public. The design must therefore consider people accessing the building from many different areas, communities and backgrounds as it is at the centre of such a variety of new and old developments. The use of the site for all times of day, and for a variety of different ages and groups of people must be considered. The building will act as community run kitchen and garden, enhancing the park’s biodiversity and act as the largest green space in Poplar. The community garden and workshop aims to educate on the process of growth and the cycle food goes through from land to plate, the economic benefit should only be to sustain the maintenance of the programme and garden. The land available on the site is not large enough to produce vegetation on an industrial scale, but this does not mean a sustainable system is not achievable. Most of the programme needs to interact with both the canal and park. A courtyard scheme connects all of the spaces without creating one large mass. Placing WCs and Thames 21 towards the road allows the building to buffer the central courtyard from the noise of the road. The bridge can link directly to the central zone connecting with a public space on the offside. Key routes through the site created a point at the centre of the site, this becomes a focal point that the bridge and accommodation should focus on. The bridge runs parallel to the tow-path at its central span to better address these key routes, whilst also creating a marina for extra mooring space whilst maintaining two way traffic through the canal. The plan addresses a central axis that connects the offside with Bartlett Park via the bridge proposal. Walkways alongside the buildings create enclosures that draw you towards the accommodation and greenhouses. Secondary axis (from Stinkhouse bridge and Poplar Union) arrive at the central axis underneath bridged walkways which connect the rooftop garden above. The scheme’s form of accommodation around greenhouses lends itself for further expansion, as and when poplar require further functions. With more development we could look at integrating more accommodation into the landscape and potentially evaluate whether a modular construction would be appropriate.
